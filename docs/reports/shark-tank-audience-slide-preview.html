Agent adoption can move faster than governance.

Blank-page friction

Sprawl and duplication

Unclear readiness

Approved Agent Lab / Ask

Preflight agents before they reach user-facing platforms.

Adopt the readiness flow

Use it as the standard path for creating or improving enterprise agents.

Stand up the Agent Lab

Create the governed workspace for versioning, testing, comparing, and reviewing agent instructions, templates, evaluations, and evidence.

Prepare the knowledge layer

Confluence for ROVO; SharePoint or equivalent knowledge pages for Copilot; reusable source packets for future platforms.

Approve agentic tooling

Provide Claude Code, Amazon Q, or equivalent approved tooling to power the Agent Lab and speed drafting, testing, comparison, and maintenance.

ROVO and Copilot are user-facing runtimes. The Agent Lab is the governed preflight workspace. Approved agentic tooling is what makes that workspace faster to operate.

Risk Management

Move deliberately where trust, visibility, and write authority matter.

Risk

Governance slows builders down

Use the lightest useful route. Prompt, automation, extension, template, candidate, new agent, and not ready are all valid outcomes.

Risk

Agents overclaim readiness

Meta-agents recommend. Humans still own Active, Ready, visibility, risky tools, and launch decisions.

Risk

Sources are stale or too broad

Require context readiness checks and named sources before durable design or broader rollout.

Risk

Pattern depends on one builder

Use backup ownership, shared review, and approved work infrastructure for the Agent Lab.

The situation

What changed

5
